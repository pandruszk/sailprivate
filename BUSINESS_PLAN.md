# SailPrivate — Business Plan

## Executive Summary

SailPrivate is a platform that enables private clubs to charter entire small luxury cruise ships for their members. We handle the complexity of ship chartering — from vessel selection to cabin sales, payment collection, and voyage coordination — so club managers can offer an extraordinary member experience without becoming travel experts.

Our initial focus is small luxury vessels (~50 cabins, 100–150 passengers) operating in the Mediterranean, with a Greece itinerary as our launch offering.

---

## The Problem

Country clubs, yacht clubs, and social clubs are always looking for premium shared experiences to strengthen member engagement and retention. A private ship charter is the ultimate version of this — but today it's nearly impossible for a club to pull off:

- **No clear starting point** — clubs don't know which ships are available for charter or how to negotiate
- **Operational nightmare** — managing 50+ cabin assignments, dietary needs, excursions, and payments across 100 members by spreadsheet
- **Financial risk** — the club must commit to the charter cost upfront and hope enough members sign up
- **No technology** — club management software (Jonas, Northstar, Clubessential) has zero tools for voyage management

Most clubs that attempt this rely on a single travel agent and a lot of manual work. Many never try at all.

---

## The Solution

SailPrivate provides a turnkey charter management platform:

1. **Ship & Itinerary Selection** — We maintain relationships with charter-ready small ship operators and present curated options to clubs
2. **Branded Booking Portal** — Each club gets a custom-branded page where members browse cabin options, select their stateroom, and pay
3. **Payment Management** — Automated deposit schedules, installment tracking, and deadline reminders
4. **Voyage Coordination** — Dietary preferences, excursion signups, onboard event planning, and member communication
5. **Fill-Rate Dashboard** — Club admins see real-time booking progress, revenue collected, and outstanding balances

| **Total** | **50** | | **$400,000** |

*Prices are per cabin (double occupancy), all-inclusive of the cruise. Members pay the club, the club pays SailPrivate.*

### Revenue Model for This Voyage

| Revenue Stream | Amount |
|---|---|
| Cabin revenue collected | $400,000 |
| Charter + operating cost | ($400,000) |
| **Gross on cabin sales** | **$0** |
| Cruise line commission (12% on $350K charter) | **$42,000** |
| Platform fee to club (optional, $0 for launch) | $0 |
| Excursion/add-on commissions (~$200/cabin) | $10,000 |
| **Net revenue per voyage** | **$52,000** |

> **Key insight:** SailPrivate prices cabins at cost to the club and members, making adoption frictionless. Revenue comes from the cruise line commission (standard 10–16% on charter value) and ancillary commissions on excursions, beverage packages, and add-ons. The club pays nothing — SailPrivate is free to them.

### Payment Schedule (per cabin)

| Milestone | Timing | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Reservation deposit | At booking | $1,000 |
| Second installment | 6 months before sailing | 50% of remaining balance |
| Final payment | 90 days before sailing | Remaining balance |

### Member Economics

- A couple pays $5,500–$14,000 for a 7-night all-inclusive private cruise in Greece
- Comparable retail price for the same cabin on a scheduled Emerald Azzurra sailing: $6,000–$18,000 per person (not per cabin)
- **Members get a 40–60% discount vs. retail** because the charter buyout is significantly cheaper than retail per-cabin rates
- The experience is exclusive — only their club, no strangers

## Revenue Model

### Primary Revenue: Cruise Line Commission
- **10–16%** of charter value, paid by the cruise line to SailPrivate as the booking agent
- Requires CLIA (Cruise Lines International Association) accreditation or partnership with an accredited host agency
- On a $350K charter: **$35,000–$56,000**

### Secondary Revenue: Ancillary Commissions
- Shore excursions: 10–20% commission
- Beverage packages, spa credits, specialty dining: 10–15% commission
- Travel insurance: 20–40% commission
- Estimated **$5,000–$15,000 per voyage**

### Future Revenue: Platform SaaS Fee
- Once value is proven, introduce an optional premium tier for clubs wanting advanced features (custom branding, CRM integration, member analytics)
- $2,000–$5,000 per voyage or $10,000–$25,000/year

### Revenue Per Voyage Summary

| Scenario | Charter Value | Commission (12%) | Ancillary | Total |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | $250,000 | $30,000 | $5,000 | $35,000 |
| Base case | $350,000 | $42,000 | $10,000 | $52,000 |
| Premium | $500,000 | $60,000 | $15,000 | $75,000 |

## Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Clubs can't fill the ship | Start with smaller vessels (50 cabins). Offer a minimum-fill guarantee structure where charter commits only after hitting 70% reservations. |
| Cruise line won't pay commission on charters | Partner with accredited host agency from day one. Charter commissions are standard industry practice. |
| Jonas/club software won't integrate | Platform works standalone — integration is a growth accelerator, not a requirement. |
| Economic downturn reduces luxury travel | Private clubs are affluent and recession-resistant. Charter pricing can flex with market conditions. |
| Legal/licensing complexity | Engage travel industry attorney. Operate under host agency umbrella initially to reduce licensing burden. |

## Licensing & Legal Requirements

- **CLIA Membership** — Required to book with most cruise lines and earn commissions
- **Seller of Travel License** — Required in CA, FL, WA, HI, IA, and several other states
- **E&O Insurance** — Professional liability coverage, standard for travel agencies
- **ARC Accreditation** — Optional but adds credibility; or operate under a host agency's ARC number
- **Business Entity** — LLC recommended
